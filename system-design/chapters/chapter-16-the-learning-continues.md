# Chapter 16: The Learning Continues

## Summary

The last chapter is a reading roadmap rather than a design exercise. It argues that strong system design judgment comes from repeated exposure to real architectures, especially postmortems and engineering write-ups from companies that operate at scale.

## Key Ideas

- Study real-world architectures to see how abstract principles are applied under business constraints.
- Look for recurring patterns across companies instead of memorizing one-off implementations.
- Read engineering blogs from target companies before interviews to learn their preferred tools and trade-offs.
- Use reference materials to deepen understanding of systems discussed in earlier chapters, such as chat, feeds, storage, caching, and ID generation.

## Interview Takeaway

The book ends by reinforcing that system design skill compounds over time. Repeated exposure to production systems is what turns interview frameworks into sound engineering judgment.
