# Chapter 3: A Framework for System Design Interviews

## Summary

This chapter is about process more than technology. Alex Xu frames the interview as a collaborative problem-solving exercise where clarity, prioritization, and trade-off awareness matter more than rushing to an answer.

## The Four-Step Framework

1. Understand the problem and establish scope.
2. Propose a high-level design and get buy-in.
3. Dive deep into the most important components.
4. Wrap up with bottlenecks, trade-offs, and future improvements.

## Key Ideas

- Do not start drawing architecture before the requirements are clear.
- Ask concrete scoping questions about scale, features, ranking rules, client types, and constraints.
- Treat the interviewer like a design partner instead of performing a monologue.
- Use box diagrams and rough numbers to validate the first-pass architecture.
- Spend the deep-dive portion on the parts that carry the most risk or complexity.
- End by acknowledging limitations, failure modes, and next iterations.

## Interview Takeaway

Good system design interviews are structured conversations. The framework keeps you from either staying too abstract or disappearing into low-value implementation detail.
